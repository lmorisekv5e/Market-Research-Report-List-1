<p><ul><li>Chemical Vapor Deposition</li><li>Liquid Impregnation Process</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The C-C composite material market encompasses various manufacturing techniques, including Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) and Liquid Impregnation Process. CVD is a technique where gaseous precursors react to form a solid material on a substrate, creating high-purity, uniform composites with excellent properties. In contrast, the Liquid Impregnation Process involves saturating a reinforcing matrix with a liquid resin or binder, enhancing structural integrity and performance. Both methods contribute to the advancements in aerospace, automotive, and defense industries, driven by their unique advantages.</p></p>
