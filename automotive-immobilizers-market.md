<p><p>The automotive immobilizers market comprises two main segments: OEM and aftermarket. OEM immobilizers are integrated into vehicles during manufacturing, enhancing security from the start and ensuring compatibility with the vehicle's systems. Aftermarket immobilizers, on the other hand, are installed separately by consumers or third-party providers, offering additional security for pre-owned vehicles or enhancing existing systems. Both types aim to prevent unauthorized vehicle access, but their installation context and user engagement differ significantly.</p></p>
